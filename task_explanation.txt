PROJECT TITLE: Books Balance Reconciliation System

PROBLEM STATEMENT:
A payments company's books don't balance at month end. They need to reconcile:
- Transactions processed on their platform (recorded instantly when customer pays)
- Actual settlements received from the bank (batched and settled 1-2 days later)

At month end, every transaction SHOULD have a matching settlement, but they don't.

OBJECTIVE:
Find out WHY the books don't balance and show where the gaps are.

================================================================================
REQUIRED GAP TYPES (Test Data Anomalies to Plant):
================================================================================

1. LATE SETTLEMENT
   - A transaction that was processed this month but settled in the FOLLOWING month
   - This creates a timing mismatch between transaction record and settlement record

2. ROUNDING DIFFERENCES
   - A rounding error that only becomes visible when summed across multiple transactions
   - May not show up in individual transaction comparisons
   - Requires data aggregation to detect

3. DUPLICATE ENTRY
   - A transaction appears TWICE in one dataset (e.g., appears 2x in platform records)
   - But only appears ONCE in the bank settlement
   - Or vice versa
   - Creates a count/amount mismatch

4. ORPHANED REFUND
   - A refund exists in one dataset
   - But the original transaction it references does NOT exist in that dataset
   - This breaks the transaction-refund pair matching logic

================================================================================
